SARAH: Ahmed, thanks for responding so quickly to my note. I know you mentioned Fatima referred you — I won't take that relationship for granted. Let me start by understanding your situation before we talk about what we do.

AHMED: Of course. The short version: we run 40 to 50 consulting engagements in parallel at any given time. Each engagement has a project manager on our side and a client sponsor on their side. The problem is that status tracking is completely decentralized. Every PM runs their project their own way — some use spreadsheets, some use email, some actually have their clients set up in Jira or Notion. My ops team spends a huge amount of time every week just trying to get a consolidated view of what's going on across all projects.

SARAH: When you say "consolidated view" — what are you actually trying to know?

AHMED: Three things mainly. First: which projects are on track, which are at risk, which are overdue. Right now I have to ask each PM individually and they're all busy. Second: client reporting. Our clients expect a status update every two weeks — professional, formatted, not a copy-paste from a spreadsheet. That takes our PMs about two hours per client per report. Multiply that by 40 clients and it's unsustainable. Third: resource allocation. I need to know where my people are committed so I can make staffing decisions. Today I have a spreadsheet that's always a week out of date.

SARAH: Those are three distinct use cases. Which one is the biggest pain — if you had to pick one?

AHMED: Client reporting. It's the most visible failure. Clients notice when reports are late or inconsistent.

SARAH: Have you evaluated any tools before?

AHMED: We looked at Monday.com and ClickUp about two years ago. Too generic — they're project management tools, not designed for consulting operations where the client is external and needs to see a polished output. We ended up not buying anything.

SARAH: That's consistent with what we hear. The client-facing reporting piece is actually one of our strongest modules. We have a report builder that auto-pulls project status data and generates a branded PDF or web report. I'd like to show you that. Do you want to go straight to a demo or do a more structured requirements session first?

AHMED: Let's do a proper requirements session. I want to make sure we capture what we actually need before we look at demos. Can we block two hours?

SARAH: Absolutely. I'll send you some options for the week of March 17.
