Meeting notes — Discovery session + live demo
Date: April 2, 2025
Location: Initech Retail HQ, Dubai
Attendees:
  Initech Retail: Priya Sharma (Head of IT), Rajan Mehta (VP Store Operations), Anna Kovacs (IT Systems Lead)
  Meridian Systems: Sarah Chen (AE), Nick Patel (Solutions Engineer)
Duration: 2 hours 15 minutes

---

CONTEXT

Initech operates 200 stores. Store managers currently manage daily operations checklists via printed forms and photograph/WhatsApp results to a regional coordinator. Opening/closing procedures, incident reporting, stock discrepancy reporting, and maintenance requests all flow through informal channels (WhatsApp groups, phone calls, email).

---

DISCOVERY — KEY FINDINGS

Rajan Mehta (VP Store Operations) led most of the requirements discussion:

"We're preparing for an RFP process. But I'll be honest — we're here today because I want to see if this is a real solution before we waste time writing requirements for something that doesn't exist in the market. Show me something real and I'll tell you if it's in scope."

Nick ran a live demo of the workflow builder, focusing on:
- Configurable daily checklists (opening/closing procedures, stock checks)
- Incident reporting with photo upload and automatic routing to regional coordinator
- Dashboard: regional coordinator sees all stores, all checklist statuses in real time
- Escalation rules: if a store hasn't completed the opening checklist by 9am, regional coordinator is automatically notified

Rajan's reaction during demo: "The checklist piece is exactly what we need. The escalation logic — that would have saved us last month when three stores opened late and nobody knew for two hours." Positive throughout.

Priya's questions (technical):
- "What ERP do you integrate with? We're on SAP S/4HANA."
- "How does access control work? Store managers should only see their store. Regional coordinators see their region."
- "Is there a mobile app for store managers? They don't have laptops."

Nick's answers:
- SAP S/4HANA: native integration, confirmed
- Access control: role-based, fully configurable — store / region / corporate hierarchy supported
- Mobile: responsive web app (no native app); Sarah noted this may or may not be sufficient — to be explored in RFP

Anna Kovacs asked about API access for custom reporting. Nick confirmed: REST API with OpenAPI spec, available on Professional and Enterprise tiers.

RFP DISCUSSION

Priya confirmed at end of session: "We will be issuing a formal RFP. Based on today, Meridian will be invited to respond. Deadline will be communicated in the RFP document. I expect to have it out by mid-April."

Sarah asked about number of vendors in the RFP. Priya: "We're inviting three vendors. We don't do wide-open RFPs."
